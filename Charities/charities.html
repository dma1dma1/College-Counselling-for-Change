Why this cause is important to us

In order to recognize the fact that there are many families that struggle on a daily basis to put food on the table as well as pay rent, we are proud to support the Brooklyn Community Foundation and its work to fight poverty and inequality.

While being the largest and most populous borough in New York City, Brooklyn is also home to much wealth disparity and racial inequality. As of 2019 the total population of Brooklyn was over two and a half million people with about 19% of those residents living below the poverty line. To add onto this, over three thousand residents of Brooklyn are imprisoned every year, 30% of renters spend more than half of their income on rent alone, and Brooklyn ranks third in the state of New York for income inequality. Due to issues like this, The Brooklyn Community Foundation has made it their vision to create, “A fair and just Brooklyn, built on dignity and respect, where all residents have the opportunity to participate and prosper.”

In order to achieve this goal and help those most in need, the Brooklyn Community Foundation works with members of its own community as well as local nonprofits in order to learn what to prioritize and how to make the most of their platform and donations. With 70% of Brooklynites being people of color, this foundation understands the need to look at these issues through a Racial Justice Lens in order to address systemic racial injustices and not overlook the issues that are heavily affected by one’s skin color.

Where your contribution will go

When making your donation, you will have the option to donate to one of five funds: the COVID-19 Response Fund, the Community Fund, the Immigrant Rights Fund, the Invest in Youth Fund, and the Racial Equity Fund.

The Racial Equity Fund focuses on initiatives that are meant to help people of color in Brooklyn determine their own sustainable future. Through the use of grantmaking, advocacy, and professional development, this foundation looks to create justice for the people of Brooklyn.

In the Invest in Youth initiative, the Brooklyn Community Foundation has donated over $2.5 million dollars annually to nonprofits. The aim of this program is to promote youth leadership and provide thousands of young people with the economic and educational opportunities that they might not otherwise have. The Foundation works to help the people of Brooklyn by working with local leaders to understand where the needs are and the resources needed to address them. By managing their resources effectively they have built their asset base and established relationships with experts in order to innovate, build on current projects and sustain their amazing work.

The COVID-19 Response Fund and Immigrant Rights Fund also seek to address systemic challenges against marginalized groups across New York City, and the Community Fund is a flexible fund to address all of the initiatives undertaken by the Brooklyn Community Foundation.

Why this cause is important to us

Long Island is amongst the 10 most racially segregated metropolitan areas in the United States, impacting housing, schooling, and diversity. During the mid-20th century, policies were put in place to ensure that mass-scale affordable housing was for white potential residents only. ERASE Racism has filed multiple civil rights complaints regarding violations made in the Affirmatively Further Fair Housing (AFFH). The AFFH is a part of the Fair Housing Act (FHA). It is intended to prevent discrimination and reverse preexisting segregation on the island.

Racial disparities in school discipline interferes with students ability to learn and negatively impact their relationship and outlook on education. In 2019, a study found that Black students are 3x more likely to be suspended or expelled when compared to their white counterparts. In conjunction with this, Black students are more likely to be removed from classrooms for subjective offenses. These statistics indicate an implicit bias on educators parts, which ERASE Racism tries to prevent or stop. In the education system, ERASE Racism promotes integration, advocating for benefits of learning environment diversity, diversity-based curriculum topics, outreach to diverse students, and the recruitment and retainment of teachers of color.

ERASE Racism’s Partnership for Racial Equity is a collective of individuals dedicated to educating themselves and others about policies and impacts of structural racism. They hold events and forums on LI to begin dialogues about and discuss action items against racial discrimination.

ERASE Racism intends on attacking issues in housing and education by spearheading public policy advocacy and other initiatives to promote racial equity on Long Island. Along with this, they participate in a wide variety of research projects, to educate and consult in activities that address structural and institutionalized racism. We have chosen to support ERASE Racism in order to aid in their goal of changing communities so that no person’s available opportunities are inhibited by their race or ethnicity. In past years, the Three Village Central School District has had several students who serve as part of ERASE Racism’s student ambassadors.

Why this cause is important to us

Many public schools around the country are underfunded, especially in marginalized and impoverished communities. These schools are unable to support their students fully, which leads students to drop out. Low-income students are less likely to graduate high school. In 2017, students in low-income families dropped out of high school at twice the rate of students in higher-income families. This education crisis in low-income communities intersects with the struggle for racial equality, as children of color are more than two times as likely as white children to be born into poverty. Students of color perform far worse on the NAEP assessment (known colloquially as “The Nation’s Report Card”) than their white counterparts. In the reading section of the NAEP, Black and Hispanic students’ average scores were 25 points below the scores of their white classmates. This difference is the equivalence of about two grade levels. The disparity in access to quality education has long-lasting impacts on the opportunities later in life that these children will encounter. Teach For America refers to this disparity as the “opportunity gap.” We have chosen to support Teach For America because its mission is to close the opportunity gap in U.S. public schools, by providing the resources and opportunities for all students to succeed.

The way TFA aims to do this is by recruiting and training adults to educate students in public schools for two years. The TFA philosophy is that there is potential in every child, and they seek out people that fundamentally believe in this principle. They currently have 6,190 members teaching 355,500 students in all grade levels and subject areas. After serving, these teachers join the TFA alumni network and continue to support TFA’s mission in their future careers. TFA creates job opportunities for these marginalized groups. Half of the people in the TFA corps identify as people of color, and half come from low-income backgrounds. Furthermore, more than 1,000 current TFA corps members had been taught by TFA teachers when they were students. TFA teaches educators to be cognizant of the obstacles that low-income students and students of color face.

How to Donate
  1. Go to the link of the charity you'd like to donate to. The links can be found in the cards on the Charities Page and also on the sign up form.
  2. On the pages, look for a button or link that says [Donate!].
  3. Donate either $30 for one session or $70 for 3 sessions. If you'd like more sessions, contact us directly at threevccc@gmail.com.
  4. After receiving the payment confirmation email, there should be a PDF attached to the email. Check for any sensitive information and forward the FULL EMAIL to threevccc@gmail.com.
  5. If there's any information you deem too sensitive to be shared, follow the instructions here to redact information. Make sure donation amount is still visible.
  6. Donation complete! We will match you with a counselor and they will reach out to you directly in your email. Make sure to check all inboxes to ensure emails don't get lost.
  7. Check out the additional resources on the Welcome Page to learn more about social activism. These issues don't go away once they stop 'trending'!
